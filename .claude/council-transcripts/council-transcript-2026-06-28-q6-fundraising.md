## Framed Question

EarningsNerd — fundraising: raise now (a), bootstrap-then-seed (b), or don't raise (c) — which fits
THIS solo, pre-revenue, pre-traffic founder; what milestone makes each credible; and the honest
narrative if raising. Built-out product, but unproven quality (#4/#7), unproven WTP (#3), no
distribution/traction (#5, the biggest blind spot), in a crowded/consolidating space. Honest
positioning = accountability, not moat (#2). Decide: path, milestone per path, amount/source if
raising, and the single most important 90-day move regardless of path.

## Advisor Responses (de-anonymized)

### The Contrarian

Don't raise now. A pre-seed raise today is a way to dodge the only test that matters — does anyone want this, and will they pay — by purchasing 18 months of permission not to find out. You'd be selling dilution on a story you've already told yourself is unproven on three axes (quality #4/#7, WTP #3, distribution #5). That's not a round; that's a loan against a fantasy.

And be honest about fundability: a solo, pre-traction founder in a *consolidating* space, against a funded leader (Fiscal.ai, 350k users) and a Microsoft-validated exit (Fintool), with a *narrower* feature set — "raise now" is the path most likely to waste the next year. Best case you don't raise and burn three months pitching. Worst case you raise $400k on a weak narrative, anchor a low valuation, and now have a board expecting a moat you've already admitted doesn't exist.

The path that fits: **bootstrap-then-seed.** The milestone that makes a seed credible isn't users — it's the fake-door WTP signal at the $39 anchor (#3) backed by eval-gated quality (#4/#7) and *one* repeatable distribution channel that isn't you posting (#5). Hit that and you're raising from strength on accountability positioning (#2), not begging on potential.

The trap in (c) "don't raise": ADR/teardowns is a lifestyle-business ceiling if it works — fine, but decide that on purpose, not by default.

Single most important thing in 90 days: **run the fake-door $39 test inside the closed beta and measure quality variance against an eval set — simultaneously.** WTP + reliability is the only data that converts any of these paths from fantasy to fundable.

### The First Principles Thinker

A solo founder pre-traction needs ~$0 to run a closed beta and a fake-door price test. Runway buys time you don't yet know how to spend.

So the question is premature. There is nothing to fund and nothing to decide. You cannot credibly raise (no traction, unproven quality, unproven WTP, crowded space, solo discount — investors price all of that at near-zero), and you'd be raising to delay learning the one thing that determines whether the business exists at all.

Path: bootstrap-then-seed — not as a strategy, but because it's the only path where the next 90 days produce the evidence that makes ANY capital decision real.

The milestone that makes each credible is the same single fact: does the wedge report reliably pass the eval gate AND do beta users hit the fake door at $39? That one resolved unknown unlocks everything — it's the precondition for "raise now," "seed from strength," or "stay solo and profitable." Until it resolves, all three paths are guesses.

Next 90 days, the only thing: ship the eval-gated "what changed, with receipts" report to ~30 hand-recruited 10-K readers, instrument the $39 fake door, and measure quality-pass-rate and click-to-pay. Come back when that number exists. Then the council has a real question.

### The Expansionist

So: **raise a $250–400k pre-seed angel round** (specialist fintech/AI angels + one operator-led micro-fund — not institutional pre-seed, who'll over-index on the solo discount). Source via warm intros from fintech operators, not cold VC.

**The milestone that makes it credible** — and the single most important thing in the next 90 days: ship the eval-gated quality fix (#4/#7) and instrument it, then run the $39 fake-door (#3) into ~50–100 closed-beta users from the ADR-teardown wedge (#1/#5). You're not raising on traction — you're raising on a **measured accuracy curve + a real WTP signal + proof you can reach the wedge.**

**Honest narrative:** "ChatGPT sells confidence; I sell verifiable, source-traced accuracy — and I'm building the only dataset that proves it. The category is consolidating around breadth; I'm going narrow-and-trusted for the investor who already reads 10-Ks, and every report makes the verification layer harder to copy. Fund the wedge before the corpus exists to fund."

Don't bootstrap-only — that caps the ceiling at lifestyle. The data asset is the venture story; build it deliberately, with capital, from day one.

### The Outsider

Not because the product is bad, but because there is literally nothing here I can underwrite except the founder's taste. The instant no for raising now: you'd be asking me to fund the discovery of whether your core artifact is even good (#4 quality unproven) and whether anyone pays (#3 WTP unproven). Milestones:

Next 90 days, regardless: run the ADR teardown distribution experiment in public. It tests #5, #3, and #4 simultaneously — and it's the only thing that makes you fundable.

### The Executor

With unproven quality and zero WTP signal, a raise-now process means 6-8 weeks of warm intros, deck iterations, and "interesting, keep us posted" — all stolen from the only thing that changes your odds: shipping a report people pay for. You'd raise a tiny, expensive, distracted round on a story you can't back.

The 90-day math for (c) "don't raise": you're already running it. Solo, pre-revenue, your burn is living costs. If you have 9-12 months of personal runway, you don't NEED money — you need PROOF. So treat the next 90 days as bootstrap whether or not you raise later.

Milestones that make each credible:
- Raise now: nothing credible yet — skip.
- Bootstrap-then-seed: 100+ engaged beta users + an eval score showing quality variance fixed + 10-20 fake-door conversions at the $39 anchor. That's a real seed deck.

If you DO raise later, it's a $150-300k pre-seed on a post-money SAFE (~$6-8M cap), from angels who invest in fintech/prosumer tools and 1-2 operator-angels who used Fiscal.ai — not YC yet (apply once you have retention). Proof-pack: 90-day retention curve + WTP fake-door + eval numbers.

Single highest-leverage 90-day action: **close the quality gate and instrument WTP in the same beta cohort** — ship the eval-gated "what changed since last quarter, with receipts" report to your closed beta, put a $39 fake-door in front of it, and measure who clicks buy. Everything else (deck, intros, accelerator) is downstream of that one number.

## Chairman's Verdict

### Where the Council Agrees

Four of five advisors — and all five peer reviewers — converge hard. The signal is unusually strong:

- **"Raise now" is the wrong path.** The Contrarian, First Principles, Outsider, and Executor all reject (a). The reasoning is identical and grounded in the spine: you'd be raising money to *de-risk the three things you can de-risk yourself for free* — quality (#4/#7), WTP (#3), distribution (#5). Capital cannot buy this specific evidence faster. As the Outsider put it from the check-writer's seat: "there is literally nothing here I can underwrite except the founder's taste." That's not a fundable round; it's a science experiment you're asking someone else to pay for.
- **Bootstrap-then-seed (b) is the structural fit** — not as a clever strategy, but because it's the only path where the next 90 days produce the evidence that makes *any* capital decision real. First Principles nailed it: "you don't have a capital problem; you have an evidence problem."
- **The milestone is a single fused number, not "users."** Every advisor independently collapsed the three unknowns into one test: does the eval-gated "what changed, with receipts" report reliably pass a quality bar AND do beta users hit a $39 fake door. One experiment, three answers.
- **The honest narrative is accountability, not moat.** Consistent with #2 — "ChatGPT sells confidence; I sell verifiable, source-traced accuracy." Nobody tried to manufacture a moat.

### Where the Council Clashes

**The real fight: (b) bootstrap-then-seed vs. (a) raise-now — and the quality of the milestone bar.**

- **The Expansionist (lone (a) vote)** argues the venture asset isn't summaries — it's the *accountability/verification corpus* (claim→source→verified datapoints), a "trust layer for filings" that compounds with usage and money genuinely accelerates. Raise $250–400k from fintech/AI angels to build it deliberately from day one; bootstrap-only caps you at lifestyle.
- **Everyone else rejects this**, and all five peer reviewers independently flagged C as the biggest blind spot. The objection is decisive: it *relitigates a decided input* (#2 — no durable moat; verification is a "feature with a head start"). A tiny labeled dataset at pre-traction scale is neither compounding nor "brutal to backfill" — a funded incumbent with 350k users (Fiscal.ai) backfills it trivially with real usage. C talks itself into raising on an asset that doesn't exist yet, which is exactly the trap the brief warned against.

**Why reasonable people disagreed here:** the Expansionist is asking the right *ambition* question (what's the biggest version?) but answering it at the wrong *time*. The corpus thesis isn't wrong forever — it's wrong *now*, pre-evidence. You can't fund a data moat before proving anyone wants the report the data comes from.

**The sharper clash — surfaced by the Outsider and confirmed by 4 of 5 peer reviews:** the milestone bar itself. Most advisors said "fake door in the closed beta." The Outsider raised the bar and won the room: **friends-and-family signups prove nothing.** The credible test isn't 50 beta signups — it's *one cold, publicly-published ADR teardown that brings strangers who convert on the $39 fake door.* That single move tests distribution (#5 — the named blind spot), price (#3), and quality (#4) simultaneously, and it's the only result a check-writer can extrapolate.

**The minor clash — (c) don't-raise:** The Contrarian warns ADR/teardowns is a "lifestyle-business ceiling" you should choose on purpose, not by default. The Executor reframes (c) as the *current* reality regardless: you're already bootstrapping; (c) and (b) are the same 90 days — the only question is whether you raise *after* the proof exists.

### Blind Spots the Council Caught

Peer review surfaced things all five advisors missed:

1. **Personal-runway math is the actual decision driver — not strategy.** Every advisor assumed the founder can self-fund 90+ days ("you need ~$0"). But if the solo founder has <6 months of living expenses, bootstrap-then-seed is *unavailable regardless of merit*, and a survival-raise is forced. The real fork is runway, not path. **No one asked how many months of cash exist.** This is the single most important unstated variable.
2. **The solo-founder discount is itself a 90-day-fixable item.** Every investor flags bus-factor and "why hasn't anyone joined you?" Three reviewers noted the council prescribed bootstrap-then-seed while ignoring the structural fix: *recruit a cofounder or a credible operator-advisor* (ideally someone who used Fiscal.ai). That directly attacks the discount before any raise.
3. **A fake-door click is intent, not WTP.** Four reviewers caught that a $39 click won't survive diligence. Real WTP needs a captured card or an actual charge attempt — a "Stripe collects card now, charge waived for beta" flow, not just a button. Demand the harder bar.
4. **No one defined a kill/pivot criterion.** What if the cold teardown test *fails*? A failed distribution test is itself a decisive, valuable signal — not just a missing fundraising input. Decide the threshold in advance.

### The Recommendation — the path, with milestones

**Path: (b) bootstrap-then-seed.** This fits THIS founder and THIS business decisively. You have an evidence problem, not a capital problem, and the next 90 days are bootstrap whether or not you ever raise. Raising now would steal 6–8 weeks of warm intros and deck iteration from the only work that changes your odds — and you'd anchor a low valuation on a story you've already admitted is unproven on three axes. Don't.

**The credibility milestone for each path — so you know what would change the call:**

- **(a) Raise now — credible at:** essentially never for this profile, *unless* one cold-published ADR teardown already pulled strangers who converted on a real $39 charge attempt, AND you've added a cofounder/operator-advisor to kill the solo discount. Absent a prior exit or warm following (you have neither), skip it.
- **(b) Bootstrap-then-seed — credible at:** eval-gated quality at a *measured* pass-rate bar (#4/#7 resolved) **+ a repeatable distribution loop** — at least one cold, public ADR teardown driving ~200+ *organic* (stranger) signups **+ 20+ real WTP signals at the $39 anchor** (captured card, not just a click). *Then* raise a **$500k–$750k seed** from angels/operator-funds on a measured accuracy curve + WTP + proof you can reach the wedge. (Not YC/institutional yet — apply once you have a retention curve.)
- **(c) Don't raise — credible at:** ~100 people paying $39 → cash-flow-positive solo. A real outcome, chosen on purpose — not the default you back into.

**The honest narrative if/when you raise (seed, from strength):**
> "ChatGPT sells confidence; I sell verifiable, source-traced accuracy — for the serious investor who already reads 10-Ks. One report: *what changed since last quarter, with the receipts.* The category is consolidating around breadth and funding; I'm going narrow-and-trusted on the accountability layer. I don't have a moat yet — at this stage the question isn't moat, it's whether I can reach the wedge faster than the category commoditizes. Here's my answer: [measured eval pass-rate], [the cold teardown that brought N strangers], [the $39 WTP signal]. I'm raising to turn one repeatable channel into ten."

**Instrument/source:** post-money SAFE; specialist fintech/prosumer angels + 1–2 operator-angels who used Fiscal.ai, via warm intros — never cold VC, who will price the solo discount at near-zero. The Executor's $150–300k pre-seed at a ~$6–8M cap is the *fallback floor* if runway forces an earlier bridge; the $500–750k seed is the target once the proof-pack exists.

**One dissent I'll endorse over the majority's framing:** the Outsider's bar beats the consensus "fake door in the closed beta." A closed-beta fake door among hand-recruited readers measures quality and intent but *not the blind spot that actually moves investors* (#5, distribution). Run the test where it counts: in public, against strangers.

### The One Thing to Do First

**Publish ONE cold, public ADR "filing teardown" built on the eval-gated "what changed since last quarter, with receipts" report — and put a real $39 fake door (card captured, charge waived) in front of the strangers it brings.**

This is the single highest-leverage move because it tests all three unknowns at once — distribution (#5, the named blind spot), WTP (#3), and quality (#4/#7) — and it's the only result that converts *any* of the three paths from fantasy to fundable. Friends-and-family signups won't do it; strangers who convert will.

Two non-negotiable instructions wrapped around it, from the peer reviews:
1. **Before you start, count your personal runway in months.** If it's under 6, that — not strategy — dictates whether you can wait for proof or must take a survival bridge. Decide that first.
2. **Set the kill criterion now.** Define in advance what teardown result (e.g., <X organic signups or <Y card captures) means "the wedge/channel doesn't work" — so a failed test is a clean pivot signal, not a sunk-cost spiral.

Come back to the council when that number exists. Then — and only then — the raise question has a real answer.
